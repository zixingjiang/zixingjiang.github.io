Research Interests
My research interests lie in achieving safe, stable, eﬃcient, innovative, minimally invasive surgery, rehabilitation, and other
medical procedures with the help of advanced engineering technologies such as robotics, artiﬁcial intelligence, image processing
and computer vision, force sensing and control, etc. Additionally, I am interested in ﬁnding inspiration from nature creatures
to optimize my robotic systems.
Keywords: Robot-assisted Medical Procedures, Image- and Force-guided Robotic System, Bio-inspired Robotics.

Selected Projects
Robot-assisted Autonomous Ultrasound for Thyroid and Breast Mar. 2023 – Oct. 2023
Participated research at RAIL, CUHK-Shenzhen, supervised by Prof. Zhenglong Sun [Link]
•
Programmed on the UR5e platform to implement some functionalities for robot-assisted automatic ultrasound, including
admittance control, automatic surface normal alignment, teleoperation, and visual servoing.
•
Applied V4L2 protocol for real-time ultrasound image acquisition and trained a U-Net for image segmentation.
•
Tested programs on thyroid and breast phantoms.
Manipulator-assisted UAV Landing on Water Surface Sept. 2020 – Feb. 2023
Participated research at RAIL, CUHK-Shenzhen, supervised by Prof. Huihuan Qian [Link-1] [Link-2]
•
Worked in a team to study the feasibility of Manipulator-assisted UAV landing on a unmanned surface vehicle (USV)
platform. Assisted in hardware design, microprocessor programming, and conducting indoor and outdoor experiments.
•
Co-proposed with a PhD student a tethered landing method to reduce the diﬃculty of landing UAVs precisely on
disturbed surface platforms in turbulent water. Fabricated a magnetic catch system to implement the proposed landing
method. This work was summarized as a conference paper presented on IEEE ICRA 2022. The system was later
deployed as the manipulator’s end eﬀector for the UAV landing assistance task. (See Link-1)
•
Co-proposed with a PhD student a motion planning algorithm based on wavelet network prediction, real-time conﬁdence
analysis, and optimal control to enable the manipulator to catch hovering UAVs on a disturbed water surface platform.
Veriﬁed feasibility and reliability of the algorithm on Simscape Multibody simulation and real robot. This work was
summarized as a journal article submitted to IEEE T-RO. (See Link-2)
Object Grasping for Manipulator on Water Surface Fall Semester 2022
Undergraduate FYP [Link]
•
Proposed a predictive control framework that makes manipulators capable of grasping objects on disturbed platforms.
The control framework consists of minimum-volume enclosing ellipsoid ﬁtting, wavelet regression based motion
prediction, cubic polynomial trajectory generation and Levenberg-Marquardt inverse kinematics.
•
Implemented the proposed control framework with MATLAB and veriﬁed its feasibility in Simscape Multibody
simulation environment.
•
Completed with Distinction, School of Science and Engineering 2019 cohort, CUHK-Shenzhen.
5-DoF Manipulator Assembly, Modeling, and Control Fall Semester 2022
Group course project (2 people), CIE6107 Robotics and Intelligent Systems, CUHK-Shenzhen [Link]
•
Assembled a 5-degree-of-freedom serial manipulator from parts (servo motors, metal frames, electronics).
•
Derived forward and inverse kinematics for the manipulator with the Denavit-Hartenberg convention. Adopted the
derived kinematics into MATLAB programs. Tested the derived kinematics on a simulated manipulator provided by the
MATLAB Robotics System Toolbox and the real manipulator.
•
Developed a remote control program for keyboards and joysticks for the manipulator with Bluetooth communication and
MATLAB-Arduino communication.
•
Achieved several functional demos including Cartesian movement of the end-eﬀector, pick-and-throw, and vision-guided
teleoperation.

Object Sorting with Eye-in-hand Manipulator Spring Semester 2022
