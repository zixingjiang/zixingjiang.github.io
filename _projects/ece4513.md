## Introduction

Odometry is wildly used to estimate position of a robot or unmanned vehicle relative to a starting location. 

$$P_{estimate}(t) = P_{start} + \int V_{measured}(t)dt$$

However, in practice, motion sensors are very likely to drift over time. So the velocity is measured with error and the integration in accumulates the error, causing inaccurate estimation. Therefore, adding extra sensors to calibrate motion sensors measurement becomes a common practice in this field. In this project, we introduced extra visual information. However, instead of traditionally using two cameras, we used a single camera to estimate the depth of an object in the scene, as shown in the figure below.

## Results

We performed a MATLAB simulation to verify the proposed pipeline. In the simulation, the camera intrinsic and extrinsic parameters, target starting position and ground truth of target velocity were all manually set[^1]. The motion sensor measurement was simulated by ground truth + Gaussian noise (20% magnitude). 

A qualitative result of the simulation is shown in the follows. The estimation of ordinary odometry drifts over time while the visual-aid odometry did make the estimation close to the ground truth.
